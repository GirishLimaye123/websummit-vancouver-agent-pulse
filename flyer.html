Which AI camp are you in?

Is the revolution already here, mostly hype, waiting for a new architecture, or risky enough to slow down?

Diffusion
Dario
The tech is basically here.
The bottleneck is whether institutions, markets, and governments absorb it fast enough.
Overhype
Gary
LLMs hit a ceiling.
Useful for coding and demos, but brittle without reasoning, world models, or neuro-symbolic systems.
Architecture
Yann
LLMs are not the endgame.
Better neural systems, world models, and JEPA-style architectures are the path to real intelligence.
Risk
Hinton
Capabilities are real enough to scare us.
The issue is not just productivity. It is control, misuse, and whether we should slow the race.
Pick one. Then tell me: what would make you switch camps?
